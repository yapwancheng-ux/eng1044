Project Milestones (13 Weeks)

This roadmap outlines our group’s key milestones and progress throughout the 13-week research project.

Week 1: Assignment Briefing

The lecturer announced that the course consists of three coursework components: the portfolio, the written response, and the research paper, along with their requirements and deadlines.

Week 2: Group Forming & Pre-research

Group members was formed and discussed which topic was suitable for the research paper.

Week 3: Find Portfolio Platform

The portfolio platform was decided to be built using HTML and CSS, with support from AI tools, and hosted on GitHub.

Week 4: Approval of Topic & Thesis Statement

A research topic was selected and a thesis statement was created and submitted. Approval was received from the lecturer.

Week 5: E-Portfolio Framework

The cover page, table of contents, and roadmap was developed for the e-portfolio.

Week 6: Gathering Sources & Constructing Outline

Reviewed journals related to the topic and created an outline with introduction, conclusion, arguments and counterarguments.

Week 7: Research Paper Outlines Consultation

Completed the research paper outline and received feedback from the lecturer..

Week 8: Individual Written Response

The individual written response was written based on the selected journals and the topic.

Week 9: Drafting Research Paper

The research paper was written with an introduction, arguments, counterargument, and conclusion, using in-text citations and a reference list in APA style.

Week 10: Research Draft Consultation

The draft was reviewed by the lecturer to check the structure of the introduction, arguments, counterargument, and conclusion.

Week 11: Research Paper Editing and Proofreading

The research paper was checked for final errors and prepared for submission.

Week 12: Sharing Session

The portfolio was shared and presented during an in-class sharing session.

Week 13: Submission (Not Completed)

The final portfolio including the cover page was submitted before the deadline.

Peer Feedback Summary

Based on feedback from 32 respondents, the research paper and portfolio was rated highly overall. Most respondents selected ratings of 4 or 5, indicating strong clarity, clear explanation of the topic, well-organised structure, and ease of understanding. The portfolio was also considered visually appealing and effective in supporting the research paper. Additionally, 96.9% agreed that the research met academic expectations, and 84.4% would recommend it to others. Areas for improvement was minimal, with a few suggestions to further simplify language and improve headings or presentation clarity.
